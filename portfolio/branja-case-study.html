Branja

Map Oriented Social Event Platform for iOS
January 2018 - December 2018

Preface

Branja is a map-oriented event platform, allowing users to search for and be invited to events based on their interests and social circles ("Branja" originates from the word tree branch, and means a large group of close friends).

This project initially started as a final assignment for a Full Stack Web Development class I took at the University of Southern California. A lot of positive feedback from friends and family drove me to take it one step further - after graduating from USC I signed up as an external stakeholder for a Computer Science capstone class and managed a team of students to develop the iOS version of Branja. For this version of Branja, I took on the role of Product Manager and Product Designer.

Development

My objective was to have a beta version of the iOS app ready for user testing by the end of the students' fall semester. I defined the core functionalities of the app within five main categories, and put together a product backlog for the students to follow as they submitted their deliverables every two weeks (in typical Agile fashion).

Core Functionalities

  • Onboarding
    • Signing up and logging into the app
  • User Profiles
    • Updating and editing the user's profile
    • Viewing another user's profile
  • Events
    • Creating and editing events
    • Events from attendee's perspective
    • Events from host's perspective
  • Search and Discovery
    • Searching for events and users
    • Map containing all the pinned events
    • Event directory with pinned events
  • Notifications
    • Friendship requests and acceptances
    • Invitations to events
    • Updates to events

Prototype

To speed up the development process, I designed a prototype using Sketch and InVision for the team's reference. I researched and analyzed several user flows of popular iOS apps (including Airbnb, Facebook, and Yelp), and adhered to contemporary UI/UX principles as I constructed the app's simple yet intuitive design. You may see some familiar design patterns found in other popular apps - this approach was intentional and in line with Jakob's (UX) Law.
